Palestinian Prisoners

Over 1 million Palestinians have been detained by Israel since 1967. Today, thousands remain imprisoned, including children, women, journalists, and political activists.

Many are held under “administrative detention,” a policy allowing indefinite imprisonment without charge or trial. This practice is widely criticized by human rights organizations.

Child detainees are often subjected to night raids, physical abuse, and coercive interrogations — all in violation of international law. Prisoners regularly face poor conditions, medical neglect, and collective punishment through solitary confinement and visitation bans.
