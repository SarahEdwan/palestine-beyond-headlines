Settlement Expansion and Land Theft

Since 1967, Israel has constructed over 250 settlements in the occupied West Bank, including East Jerusalem. These settlements are considered illegal under international law, particularly the Fourth Geneva Convention.

The expansion of these settlements often results in the forced displacement of Palestinian families, land confiscation, and destruction of olive groves — vital to Palestinian livelihood. Settler-only roads divide Palestinian communities, and violence from settlers is frequently carried out with impunity.

Settlement growth undermines the possibility of a two-state solution by fragmenting Palestinian land into isolated enclaves.
