Apartheid

Numerous human rights organizations — including Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and Israeli group B’Tselem — have concluded that Israeli policies toward Palestinians amount to apartheid.

This includes systematic segregation, denial of basic rights, movement restrictions, discriminatory laws, and unequal access to resources like land, water, and infrastructure.

Palestinians in the occupied territories live under military law, while Israeli settlers in the same areas are subject to civilian law. Within Israel, Palestinian citizens face systemic discrimination in housing, employment, and education.

The result is a dual legal system based on ethnicity — a hallmark of apartheid.
